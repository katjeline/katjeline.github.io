Golden Akhal Teke

The Akhal Teke has a shiny coat that appears to be metallic in the sun. It is beautiful enough to be a bronzed statue. The Akhal Teke is most famous for their shining coats, and can also be colored black, palomino, perlino, grey, and cremello.

The Friesian/Appaloosa crossbreed

Mystic Warrior is 3/4 Frisian and 1/4 Appy Stallion. He has been famous ever since a picture of him as a foal surfaced online. He was a demo horse at the World Equestrian Games. They want him to go to a good home that will keep him a stallion, so he can pass his traits and colors on.

The Knabstrupper

Knabstruppers were bred in Danmark till 1750 when the royal bleedline came to an end. The horse returned in 1812 as a new bloodline. A mare purchased from Spain had silver-dollar sized markings, and was the foundater of the new Knabstruppers.

The German Black Forest horse

The Black Forest Horse is a rare horse breed originating in Southern Germany. It has a dark brown coat with long blond manes.

The Blue Roan Gypsy Vanner horse

The Gypsy Vanner has feathered legs, and common black, white or "piebald" coat color. The Blue Roan version of this horse is the most rare.

The Chocolate Silver Dapple Pinto

Austin is a unique show horse. He has lush manes and an extrordinary bold coat, but the most unique are the star-shaped dapples on his front end.

The Friesian-Gypsy Vanner crossbreed

With the beautiful feathered legs and coat color of the Gypsy Vanner, and the long manes and shining black coat of a Frisian this horse is truly beautiful.

The Sorraia Mustang

The Sorraia Mustang is an endangered South Iberian wild horse characterized by a coat that is somewhere between grey and brown.

The Cremello Tennessee Walking horse

This horse is not as rare as some of the other horses, but is a blond beauty with beautiful baby blue eyes.
